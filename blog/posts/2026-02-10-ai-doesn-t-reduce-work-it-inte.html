The High-Speed Burnout Trap

In logistics, speed is everything. But there’s a hidden cost to the AI revolution that no one is talking about. It’s called Workload Creep.

When you give a planner or a warehouse manager an AI tool, they don't take a longer lunch. They don't go home at 5 PM. Instead, they fill that "saved" time with three more tasks. They expand their scope. They start answering emails at midnight because the AI made it "easier" to keep going.

This isn't productivity. It's a death march for your margins.

What We Discovered

New research proves that AI tools consistently intensify work rather than reducing it. Your team is redlining their engines. They are taking on more responsibility without being asked. Eventually, the engine blows.

  • Decision Fatigue: More data means more choices. More choices lead to $20,000 routing mistakes and missed deliveries.
  • The Turnover Tax: Replacing a senior logistics manager costs 2x their annual salary. AI is making your best talent want to quit today.
  • Quality Decay: Fast work is often sloppy work. Your customers will notice the drop in service long before you see it in your spreadsheets.

The 18-Month Prediction

The winners won't use AI to squeeze more hours out of people. They will use it to create intentional pauses and human grounding in their supply chain. They will prioritize sustainable output over a 30-day "productivity surge" that kills morale.
